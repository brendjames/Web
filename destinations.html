South Africa

Overview

Experience the diverse coastline of South Africa, offering everything from world-renowned point breaks like Jeffrey's Bay to beginner-friendly beach breaks in Cape Town. A vibrant surf culture, stunning landscapes, and thrilling wildlife encounters await.

Iconic Surf Spots

  • Jeffrey's Bay (J-Bay): World-class right-hand point break, a mecca for experienced surfers.
  • Muizenberg (Cape Town): Gentle, rolling waves perfect for beginners and longboarders, set against colorful beach huts.
  • Elands Bay: Remote and rugged left-hand point break, known for its consistency and power.
  • Durban: Warm Indian Ocean waters and consistent beach breaks for all levels.

Best Time to Surf

Generally, March to September offers prime conditions for J-Bay and the Cape Peninsula. Durban provides enjoyable year-round surfing.

Accommodation Options

From stylish boutique hotels in Cape Town and surf lodges in J-Bay to charming guesthouses along the Garden Route, South Africa caters to every traveler's preference and budget.

Beyond the Waves

  • Embark on a thrilling safari adventure in Kruger National Park.
  • Explore the vibrant city of Cape Town, hike Table Mountain, or visit Robben Island.
  • Indulge in world-class wine tasting in the Stellenbosch and Franschhoek regions.
  • Experience shark cage diving (for the truly adventurous!).

Bali, Indonesia

Overview

Known as the "Island of the Gods," Bali is a tropical paradise offering consistent world-class waves, a rich and vibrant culture, and a famously welcoming atmosphere for surfers of all levels.

Iconic Surf Spots

  • Uluwatu: Legendary left-hand reef break, offering multiple peaks for intermediate to advanced surfers.
  • Kuta Beach: Bustling beach break, perfect for beginners to learn and progress.
  • Canggu: A trendy surf hub with a variety of reef and beach breaks suitable for different levels.
  • Padang Padang: A challenging world-class left-hand reef break, home to the prestigious Rip Curl Cup.

Best Time to Surf

The dry season (May to September) is generally optimal for most iconic west coast breaks like Uluwatu. The wet season (November to March) can offer good conditions for east coast spots.

Accommodation Options

Choose from luxurious private villas in Seminyak, traditional bungalows in Canggu, lively surf camps in Kuta, or serene eco-lodges nestled in quieter parts of the island.

Beyond the Waves

  • Explore ancient sea temples like Tanah Lot and Uluwatu Temple at sunset.
  • Immerse yourself in yoga and wellness retreats in the spiritual heart of Ubud.
  • Discover Balinese culture through its art, dance, and stunning rice paddies.
  • Enjoy world-class diving and snorkeling in nearby islands like Nusa Lembongan.
